UK Wildlife Law

This page from The Wildlife Trusts website focuses on raising awareness about UK wildlife protection laws and encouraging public action to support conservation. It outlines key legislation such as the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 and the Animal Welfare Act 2006, that safeguard animals, habitats, and ecosystems.

Footprint Calculator

The WWF Footprint Calculator is an interactive tool that helps users measure their personal impact on the environment based on lifestyle choices around food, travel, home, and consumption. By answering a series of simple questions, users receive a carbon footprint estimate, a comparison to the UK average, and practical tips for reducing their impact. Designed to be educational and user-friendly, the calculator raises awareness of how individual actions contribute to global issues like climate change and biodiversity loss. It encourages reflection and empowers users to make more sustainable choices.

Extinction: The Facts

Extinction: The Facts is a documentary presented by Sir David Attenborough that explores the urgent global crisis of biodiversity loss, with over a million species at risk of extinction. It reveals how this crisis affects not just wildlife, but also human well-being, threatening food and water security, destabilising the climate, and increasing the risk of pandemics. The documentary highlights how everything in nature is interconnected, and the loss of species disrupts essential systems like crop pollination and soil health. It identifies the destruction of natural habitats mostly for agriculture, as the leading cause of biodiversity loss, driven by consumer demand. Ultimately, it warns that unless we change our relationship with nature, the consequences for both ecosystems and humanity will be severe.
